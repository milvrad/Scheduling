🚨 Call, Nighthawk, WebExchange, and Urgent Coverage

Handle call priorities, NHWK schedule maintenance, public on-call consistency, and urgent coverage gaps.

WhatWhyWhat to useHow to use itSelf-check
Module progress:

Learning objectives

  • Apply call scheduling priority order.
  • Explain why WebExchange must match QGenda for public-facing on-call schedules.
  • Use the urgent coverage change sequence.
  • Recognize standard NHWK time patterns and exceptions that must be checked against the current night calendar.

Why this matters

  • Call and urgent coverage gaps can affect patient care, contrast/procedure support, site expectations, and public-facing contact information.
  • When QGenda and WebExchange disagree, users may rely on the wrong schedule.

What to use

  • QGenda for live coverage assignments and schedule changes.
  • WebExchange for NHWK, Peds, NM, and other public-facing call listings.
  • Current night calendar and current quarter NHWK email/source.
  • Tiger Connect scheduler role for urgent current-day/next-day coverage issues.
  • Call counts and prior evening/weekend context for fairness.

How to use it

  1. For call scheduling, assign hard-to-fill days first, especially Friday and Monday.
  2. Then assign radiologists with the fewest call counts.
  3. Use prior evening shifts when possible to balance workload and work-life burden.
  4. Avoid assigning call immediately before vacation or directly onto weekend workers unless unavoidable and approved.
  5. For NHWK, check the current night calendar before applying start/stop times or 7-on/14-off patterns.
  6. Maintain NHWK in both QGenda and WebExchange when scheduled for the current week.
  7. For an urgent coverage gap, identify the gap, find eligible coverage in QGenda, text the coverer using the correct role/group, email affected sites or leaders as required, update QGenda immediately, and update WebExchange/tracking/payroll if affected.
  8. Document the exact date, time, task, coverage details, and downstream updates.

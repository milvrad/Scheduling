🧭 Role, Safety, and Source of Truth

Understand the scheduler role, the operational source of truth, and the daily rule that keeps coverage safe.

WhatWhyWhat to useHow to use itSelf-check
Module progress:

Learning objectives

  • Explain the scheduler’s core responsibility in one sentence.
  • Identify which source controls the live schedule.
  • Describe what must happen before a schedule decision is complete.
  • Recognize why outdated copies and unsupported preferences are not safe scheduling sources.

Why this matters

  • Radiology coverage depends on multiple systems and multiple sites. A small mismatch can create a missed reader, uncovered call, inaccurate public on-call listing, incorrect payroll entry, or incorrect site expectation.
  • The safest scheduling habit is to finish every decision the same way: QGenda reflects the plan, the correct people are notified, and any needed notes are visible in the schedule.

What to use

  • QGenda: live schedule, task assignments, notes, stats, request limits, publishing, and tracking.
  • Current quarter folders: weekend schedule, days-off grid, tumor board documents, nuclear medicine weekends, and seasonal calculations.
  • Paycom/contract/onboarding information: restrictions, availability, start status, employment type, call/weekend/holiday obligations, and proration.
  • SharePoint/MILV Intranet: policies, contact lists, meeting/event calendars, and current scheduling resources.
  • Email/Tiger Connect: communication channel, not the final schedule record.

How to use it

  1. Start with the current operational source for the decision you are making.
  2. Confirm whether the decision affects today, tomorrow, a published schedule, a request limit, a public on-call listing, or payroll/reconciliation.
  3. Verify that the person is eligible for the shift by checking specialty, site, contract/availability, onboarding status, and any restriction that applies.
  4. Enter or update the assignment in QGenda. Add notes for home, remote, seating, request date, special coverage, or location details when needed.
  5. Notify the correct recipients using the routine or urgent communication pathway.
  6. Update connected tracking systems such as WebExchange, payroll/extra work, annual reconciliation, or Admin Track/Untrack when applicable.
  7. Before moving on, re-check QGenda to confirm the schedule shows what you intended.

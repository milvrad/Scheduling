Module 11 • Tracking and fairness

🧾 Payroll, Extra Work, Reconciliation, and Provider Checks

Document extra work, paid coverage, weekend points, admin/residency days, swaps, and provider eligibility accurately.

WhatWhyWhat to useHow to use itSelf-check
Module progress:

Learning objectives

  • Explain why payroll and reconciliation tracking must match schedule changes.
  • Distinguish employee extra shifts from independent contractor tracking.
  • Update annual reconciliation after weekend schedule completion.
  • Recognize provider status items that must be verified before scheduling.

Why this matters

  • A schedule can look correct operationally but still be wrong for payroll, annual reconciliation, weekend fairness, or contract compliance.
  • Provider status and restrictions change. New hires, 75% radiologists, delayed starts, FMLA, retirements, and contractors require extra verification before assignment.

What to use

  • Extra Work Days list and payroll worksheet.
  • Annual reconciliation files for partners, employees, and 75% radiologists.
  • Weekend Days Off Grid and points logic.
  • Admin/residency tracking workbook.
  • Paycom/contract/onboarding records for provider eligibility and obligations.
  • QGenda Admin Track/Untrack after swaps.

How to use it

  1. Watch for $ symbols, sold shifts, volunteer coverage, and extra work assignments when reviewing weekend and schedule documents.
  2. Document paid, sold, or extra work in the correct payroll/reconciliation location.
  3. Track employees and independent contractors separately when the workflow requires separate tracking.
  4. After the weekend schedule is complete, add weekend points to annual reconciliation files using 1 point for half-day, 2 points for full-day, and 2 points for 4-12 difficulty when applicable.
  5. For swaps, move tracking from the original radiologist to the receiving radiologist in QGenda Admin.
  6. Before assigning a new or status-sensitive provider, verify start date, onboarding readiness, system access, contract, availability, restrictions, weekend/call/holiday obligations, and proration needs.
  7. Do not schedule a provider simply because the name appears in a list; verify the provider is cleared and eligible for the assignment.

🪑 Evenings, Seating, and Site Coverage

Learn how evening staffing, sitting coverage, fixed sites, and location notes prevent coverage gaps.

WhatWhyWhat to useHow to use itSelf-check
Module progress:

Learning objectives

  • Explain why two sitting radiologists are required every evening.
  • Use seating notes for Palmer, Cetronia, LVI-1247, LVI-1220, and Muhlenberg Bathgate when applicable.
  • Recognize fixed/recurring placements and always-evening patterns.
  • Identify when a site-specific assignment should not be mixed into a general workflow task.

Why this matters

  • Seating is not just preference. Some sites need physical or designated coverage for contrast, procedures, evening needs, and patient safety.
  • Notes make the location expectation visible to radiologists and downstream teams.

What to use

  • Weekly seating chart and current seating list.
  • Schedule Check Grid for fixed locations and sitting needs.
  • QGenda notes for seating/location details.
  • Current site coverage reference for contrast/procedure coverage structures.
  • Evening seating rules and day-of-week guidance.

How to use it

  1. Check the weekly seating chart before filling evening and site-specific assignments.
  2. Confirm two radiologists are sitting in the department every evening.
  3. Apply known seating notes when assigning Palmer, Cetronia, LVI-1247, LVI-1220, and Muhlenberg Bathgate.
  4. For Muhlenberg Bathgate, use the number of Neuro radiologists at Muhlenberg to determine whether Neuro or Body covers AM/PM Bathgate.
  5. Check Thursday evening Bathgate restrictions so a radiologist with CC Fluoro or LVI Fluoro is not assigned inappropriately.
  6. Keep site-specific assignments such as Nighthawk, Peds, MSK, Neuro, Delta Medics/Steamtown, and WebExchange on-call entries separate from general workflow tasks.
  7. Add blocker tasks when the assignment should remove the radiologist from the general staffing pool.
  8. Re-check the QGenda display and notes after seating assignments are entered.
